Equation \begin{align*} \dot{x} &= f(x,y) \\\dot{y} &= \frac{1}{\epsilon} g(x,y) \end{align*} Perturb \begin{align*} y&: \eta_0(x)\to \eta_0(x)+\epsilon\eta_1(x) \\\dot{y}&\to \dot{\eta_0}(x) + \epsilon\dot{\eta_1}(x) \\&\sim \partial_x \eta_0\cdot \dot{x} \\&\sim \partial_x \eta_0\cdot f(x,\eta_0) \\\frac{1}{\epsilon} g(x,y)&\to \frac{1}{\epsilon} g(x,\eta_0+\epsilon\eta_1) \\&\sim \frac{1}{\epsilon}(g(x,\eta_0) + \epsilon\eta_1\cdot \partial_y g(x,\eta_0)) \\&= \eta_1 \cdot \partial_y g(x,\eta_0) \end{align*} Equating both sides \begin{align*} \partial_x \eta_0\cdot f(x,\eta_0) &= \eta_1 \cdot \partial_y g(x,\eta_0) \\\Rightarrow \eta_1 &= (\partial_y g(x,\eta_0))^{-1} \partial_x \eta_0\cdot f(x,\eta_0) \end{align*} Reduced approximation \begin{align*} \dot{X} &= f(x,\eta_0) + \epsilon\eta_1\cdot \partial_y f(x,\eta_0) \\&= f(x,\eta_0) + \epsilon(\partial_y g)^{-1} \partial_x \eta_0\cdot f(x,\eta_0)\cdot \partial_y f \end{align*} Interpretation: \(\dot{y} \sim \partial_x\eta_0\cdot \dot{x}\), \(\dot{y}\sim \eta_1\cdot \partial_y g\)
